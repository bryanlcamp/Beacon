Author

Dataset Creation & Market Simulation Configuration

Overview

Author is Beacon's dataset creation engine, designed to generate realistic market scenarios that break free from historical overfitting. Instead of training strategies on the same March 15th sequence that teaches March 15th quirks rather than market behavior patterns, Author creates infinite variations of market conditions.

Core Capabilities

Product Configuration

Define product symbols, tick sizes, lot sizes, and trading sessions with exchange-specific parameters.

Microstructure Design

Configure spread dynamics, volatility regimes, and order flow patterns that mirror real market behavior.

Multi-Format Export

Generate datasets in CME MDP, NASDAQ ITCH, and NYSE formats with nanosecond precision.

Scenario Scaling

Transform one quiet Tuesday into fifty unique quiet Tuesdays, each with realistic variance.

How It Works

1. Pattern Specification

Define the fundamental market patterns you want to simulate—volatility clusters, spread compression events, liquidity gaps, or momentum breaks. Author understands these as structural behaviors, not specific price sequences.

2. Parameter Ranges

Set realistic ranges for key microstructure parameters including:

  • Bid-ask spread dynamics and mean reversion
  • Order arrival rates and size distributions
  • Volatility clustering and regime changes
  • Cross-correlation with related products

3. Realistic Generation

Author generates market data that preserves the statistical properties and structural relationships that matter for strategy development, while introducing the natural variance that prevents overfitting.

Integration with Beacon Ecosystem

Datasets created in Author flow seamlessly into the broader Beacon platform:

  • Pulse broadcasts the generated data with exchange-accurate timing
  • Core executes strategies against the realistic market conditions
  • Match simulates venue-specific latency and execution quirks

Getting Started

  1. Access the Author interface through the Beacon command center
  2. Select your target exchange format (CME, NASDAQ, or NYSE)
  3. Configure product specifications and trading sessions
  4. Define market microstructure parameters
  5. Generate and export your datasets

Author addresses the fundamental challenge in quantitative trading: how do you test strategies against market conditions that feel real but haven't happened yet? The answer is generating market behavior that preserves structure while avoiding the trap of memorizing history.
