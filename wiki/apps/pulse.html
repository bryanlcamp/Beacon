Pulse

Real-Time Market Data Broadcasting Engine

Overview

Pulse is Beacon's market data distribution engine that delivers realistic market scenarios with exchange-accurate timing. It bridges the gap between simulated datasets and live trading environments, ensuring your strategies experience market data with the same temporal characteristics they'll encounter in production.

Core Capabilities

Exchange-Accurate Timing

Maintains nanosecond precision timing that mirrors real exchange delivery patterns and jitter.

Multicast UDP Delivery

High-performance network distribution using optimized multicast protocols for minimal latency.

Liquidity Gap Injection

Introduce realistic market stress scenarios including sudden liquidity withdrawals and momentum breaks.

Real-Time Fill Confirmations

Strategy execution confirmations delivered with realistic venue latency and order lifecycle timing.

Technical Architecture

Performance Specifications

  • Sub-microsecond message processing latency
  • Support for 10M+ messages per second
  • NUMA-aware memory allocation for consistent performance
  • Zero-copy networking stack integration

How It Works

1. Dataset Loading

Pulse ingests datasets created by Author or historical market data, parsing message formats and extracting timing metadata. It builds an internal representation that preserves both order book states and temporal relationships.

2. Timing Reconstruction

The engine reconstructs realistic timing patterns including:

  • Network jitter and delivery variance
  • Exchange processing delays and batching
  • Cross-venue timing skews and synchronization
  • Market stress amplification effects

3. Real-Time Delivery

Market data streams to subscribers with production-level characteristics, allowing strategies to experience realistic timing pressures and network effects during development and testing.

Market Scenario Control

Liquidity Events

Pulse can inject controlled liquidity stress scenarios:

  • Sudden spread widening events
  • Market maker withdrawals
  • Momentum gap scenarios
  • Cross-market arbitrage opportunities

Execution Feedback

When strategies submit orders through Core, Pulse provides realistic fill confirmations with venue-specific characteristics including partial fills, latency variance, and rejection scenarios.

Integration Points

Pulse sits at the center of the Beacon ecosystem:

  • Author → provides market datasets with embedded timing metadata
  • Core → receives ultra-low latency market data feeds
  • Match → coordinates execution confirmations and venue modeling

Getting Started

  1. Load your dataset (from Author or historical sources)
  2. Configure timing parameters and stress scenarios
  3. Set up multicast groups for strategy subscribers
  4. Initiate real-time market data broadcasting
  5. Monitor delivery metrics and strategy performance

The critical insight behind Pulse is that market data timing isn't just about speed—it's about realistic variance. Your strategy needs to work when the market data arrives 50 microseconds late, or when three tickers arrive bunched together after a brief gap. Pulse ensures you're prepared for production timing realities.
